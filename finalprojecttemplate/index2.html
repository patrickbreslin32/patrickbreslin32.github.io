Who is the NFL MVP Frontrunner After Week 13?

By Patrick Breslin

At the conclusion of every National Football League (NFL) season, a panel of 50 sportswriters assess players' performances and contributions throughout the regular season. individual brilliance is celebrated through a series of awards that honor outstanding performances and contributions. The most prestigious honors include the Most Valuable Player (MVP), Offensive and Defensive Player of the Year, Offensive and Defensive Rookie of the Year, and more. Every NFL award has its own set of criteria designed to honor achievement in a particular area of the game. Voters take into account a variety of factors, including statistical accomplishments, impact on team performance, leadership traits, and overall contribution to the game, while evaluating offensive prowess, defensive mastery, rookie promise, or sportsmanship.

Experts, journalists, and former players make up the panel that selects the NFL awards, and they vote according to preset standards. This guarantees a thorough assessment of applicants in every category. Different awards may have different voting procedures, such as direct player or coach voting or ranking systems, with the highest number of votes cast determining the winner. For example, Kansas City Chiefs star quarterback, Patrick Mahomes, received 48 out of 50 first place votes for the MVP award. Voters credited Mahomes for guiding the Chiefs to the best record in the league (14-3) while providing astronomical statistics: Mahomes threw for a league-leading 41 touchdowns and 5,250 passing yards.

So far in the 2023 NFL Season, there has been much debate over who is deserving of this award. Miami Dolphins Quarterback Tua Tagovailoa was the favorite for the award in the first half of the season, as he led the Dolphins’ offense to a historic start, which included a dominant 70 point outing versus the Denver Broncos in Week 3. San Francisco 49ers Running Back Christain McCaffrey has produced a dominant season, leading the league in rushing yards and touchdowns scored. McCaffrey’s value stems from his versatility: he is such a strong runner, defenses are frequently forced to play eight in the box. Moreover, most defenses have no linebacker or safety that can cover McCaffrey man-to-man since he is such a superb receiver that he forces them to play zone coverage most of the time. Thus, he also facilitates the lives of his teammates.

Buffalo Bills Quarterback Josh Allen has had an excellent season, throwing for 24 touchdowns through 12 weeks. However, his name isn’t mentioned with the other favorites due to his team record. The Buffalo Bills are currently 6-6 and out of the playoff picture, but upcoming games against the Kansas City Chiefs and Dallas Cowboys can bolster Allen’s case for the final stretch of the season. The other marquee MVP candidate is Philadelphia Eagles Quarterback Jalen Hurts. Hurts, who came second in MVP voting last year, has 31 total touchdowns while leading the Eagles to a 10-2 record, the best in the NFL.
